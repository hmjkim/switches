What is a Switch?

A mechanical keyboard switch is a physical switch mechanism that is placed underneath every key on the keyboard.It records and registers each keystroke. These switches have a variety of response, noise, and travel times.

Anatomy of a Switch

  1. Upper Housing

    The part where the keycap hits when pressing the key. Protects the stem and helps with precision guidance.

  2. Stem

    Helps regulate the keystroke feel and determines the switch type.

  3. Crosspoint Contact

    Carries the electrical flow needed to alert the circuit board that a key has been pressed. Guides the switch to slide back to its original position.

  4. Coil Spring

    Determines the amount of vertical pressure needed to actuate the key.

  5. Bottom Housing

    The part where the upper housing is mounted and where the actual installation of the switch to the keyboard happens.

Types of Switches

Linear

Executes simplest operation that moves straight up and down without any tactile feedback or clicking noise.

Smooth and Direct

Tactile

Provides a notiecable bump in the middle of travel to let you know that the key press has been registered.

Bumpy and Noticeable

Clicky

Produces a tactile bump and creates distinct audible clicky sound with each keystroke.

Clicky and Loud
